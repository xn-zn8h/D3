Controls
The player moves using the keyboard and attacks using the mouse.

Example Gameplay
The game begins in an empty room with several exits. After walking through one of the doors, the player appears in another room where enemies are waiting. The player can attack enemies but can also bypass them and move on.

During gameplay, the player may find an urn that can be attacked. After being attacked, the urn adds an item from the item pool to the player's inventory and then disappears.

The player can activate an item to apply its effect, depending on the item.

After cumulatively defeating 75% of enemies on a floor, upon defeating the next enemy, the player receives a key that allows them to advance to the next floor. The player must find the passage that leads to the next floor.

During gameplay, the player can freely move between rooms on a floor, but after advancing to the next floor, they cannot return to the previous one.

After reaching the second floor, gameplay starts similarly to a new game, but the starting room contains an NPC. The NPC has a random inventory to trade with the player.

Item Pool
After breaking an urn, the player can receive:
- Stat boost (attack / health / armor)
- Random amount of coins
- Special item

Special Items
Special items temporarily affect gameplay and can be activated by the player at any time. Example special items include:
- Armor boost for 3 minutes
- Weaken enemies on the floor
- Increase player movement speed for 3 minutes
- Invisibility – enemies do not attack the player until they perform an attack, get too close, or leave the room. (Rare effect for the hardest rooms, does not work on "bosses" if implemented)
- Healing
- Activatable item that freezes all enemies in the current room for 5 seconds. They cannot move or attack. They also do not immediately take damage. Instead, damage is stored by the game. After the effect expires, they receive all the damage that was dealt to them while frozen.

Difficulty Scaling
Each subsequent floor should be harder than the previous one, through:
- Number of enemies
- Enemy AI advancement
- Higher health / armor / damage stats for enemies

Combat
Combat consists of dodging telegraphed enemy attacks and attacking them in the windows between attacks. During "charging" of attacks, the enemy stands still and is vulnerable to attacks. Good timing allows for faster enemy defeats. Poor timing punishes the player with taken damage. Knockback can also be added, which will cause the player to be knocked away after being attacked, losing the ability to attack until they return to the enemy.
Example enemy attacks:
- Dash – a rectangle appears in front of the enemy symbolizing the attack area. Arrows » appear above the enemy, symbolizing this attack type. After a set time, the enemy makes a fast movement to the end of the previously signaled rectangle, dealing damage to the player if they are in the attack area.
- Spin – a circle appears around the enemy. After a set time, the enemy spins around its own axis, dealing damage to the player if they are in the attack area.
- Fireball – the enemy stops briefly. A stationary circle appears around the player symbolizing the attack area. The player must leave this area – otherwise they will take damage (and optionally receive the "on fire" status effect. While this effect is active, the player will continuously take damage. To remove this status, the player must press a button that appears after receiving this status. After pressing this button, the player character performs a "stop, drop and roll" which immobilizes them in place for 3 seconds, or another duration, as they try to extinguish themselves.)

Player Attacks:
The player's attack mechanics primarily revolve around white weapon (melee) attacks. This attack is performed by a quick left mouse click. This attack deals a set amount of damage to an enemy within the attack cone, which is directed toward the mouse position. It is affected by the attack stat.

A "ranged" attack is also possible. To perform it, the player must hold the left mouse button. (After a few milliseconds of holding, a charge bar should appear above the player indicating attack charge progress. If released early, the player performs a standard melee attack. If fully charged, the player character fires a projectile. Upon colliding with an enemy, the projectile applies the "electrified" effect for 5 seconds. This effect does nothing upon expiration unless the player hits the enemy. When dealing damage to an enemy with the "electrified" effect, the enemy takes 50% more damage from that attack and the effect is removed. Damage with this effect increases to 75% more for backstab attacks.)

Optional NPC Upgrades:
NPCs offer upgrades in exchange for coins. On every second floor (or every non-first floor, or every floor, depending on balance), one NPC appears in the starting room and offers three upgrades, from which the player can choose one. After purchasing an upgrade, the others become unavailable.
Upgrades the player can purchase:
- Increase attack cone size
- Increase attack speed
- Increase movement speed by 5%
- Replace ranged attack with one that slows enemy movement and attacks instead of applying "electrified"
- Replace ranged attack with one that works like the enemies' "Dash" attack
- Melee damage heals the player for 10% of dealt damage
- One-time player healing
- Item (like those from urns) that heals the player
- Activatable item that increases movement speed by 25% for three minutes but also increases received damage by 200%
- Passive effect that causes the player to recover 15% of max HP after taking damage that would have been fatal (can trigger multiple times)
- Passive effect that causes the player to receive 10% more coins every time they would earn coins. Effect can stack.

Bossfight
After clearing 5 floors, the 6th floor has two rooms. One – where the player spawns after entering this floor – contains an NPC. The NPC trades normally but warns that a dangerous enemy is in the adjacent room.
In the adjacent room waits an enemy with significantly more health and attack than normal enemies. It can also attack using a mix of different attack types from normal enemies. These attacks would have larger areas, shorter reaction times, and higher damage. Additionally, this enemy is capable of special attacks (todo)
